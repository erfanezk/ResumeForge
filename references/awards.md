# Awards Deep Dive

## Why They Matter

Awards highlight accomplishments and help you stand out. They show recognition for hard work and excellence. Be selective and strategic about which to include.

## Where to List Them

- **Education Section**: Academic awards like scholarships, Dean's List, honors. Especially relevant if early in career.
- **Work Experience Section**: Job performance awards listed under the relevant job. Contextualizes achievements.
- **Dedicated Awards Section**: If you have multiple or particularly prestigious awards, create a separate "Awards" or "Honors" section.

## How to Format

Each award listing should include:
- **Name of the Award**: Clearly state it
- **Date Received**: Month and year
- **Awarding Body**: Organization or institution that granted it
- **Reason or Achievement**: Briefly explain why you received it, especially if not self-explanatory

## Rules

- ✅ Only relevant awards or honors related to your field
- ✅ Include award name, awarding organization, and date
- ✅ Only include official awards accepted worldwide
- ❌ Don't include irrelevant or insignificant awards
- ❌ Don't list awards from a long time ago unless particularly impressive or relevant to current work
