# Resume Structure Deep Dive

## Content-wise vs Visualization-wise

There are different categories of resumes based on content style and visual layout. The combination produces different structures.

## General Formatting Guidelines

- ✅ Name and job title at the top, big fonts
- ✅ Black font for body text, titles can use another color
- ✅ Save resume with your name and the date generated (e.g., JohnDoe_2024-01-15)
- ✅ Position first, then company name. Same for education: degree subject first, then university name
- ✅ Date format: MM/YYYY consistently throughout
- ✅ 475-600 words total
- ✅ Bullet points for all text except summary
- ✅ Font sizes: 11-12pt body, 14-16pt headers
- ✅ 1 page if under 7-10 years experience, 2 pages otherwise
- ✅ Proper spacing — sections recognizable at a glance
- ✅ Include relevant experience even if unpaid
- ✅ Include summary/objective at the top
- ✅ No overlapping dates (except work + education)
- ✅ Most recent position first, work backward
- ❌ Don't list every job — only relevant experience
- ✅ Emphasize unique related value proposition or memberships
- ✅ Minimum 2 bullet points, maximum 6-7 per job

## Example Structure (Chronological)

1. Personal data
2. Summary
3. Skills
4. Working experience
5. Education
6. Other optional sections (projects, awards, certificates, etc.)
7. Referral letter (usually on second page)

**Order within sections**: By date, most recent entry on top.

## Content-wise Styles

### Chronological
Lists work experience in reverse chronological order with achievements per role. Traditional, easy to read. May not be ideal for candidates without employment history or career changers.

### Functional
Emphasizes base skills over work experience. Useful for career changers or those with no work experience. **Avoid if possible** — not ATS-friendly, and HR/technical reviewers prefer candidates who can present core job skills.

### Combination
Combines chronological and functional elements. Highlights both skills and work experience. Better than functional but still less common than chronological. May fit candidates with limited working experience who struggle to fill a one-column chronological resume.

## Visualization Styles

### One Column
One section per row. Clear, easy to read. Modern and visually appealing. Single column layout keeps information organized with clear section headings, easy for recruiters to scan.

### Two Column
Two sections per row. More compact but can be harder to scan quickly and may cause ATS parsing issues.

**Recommendation**: One column is generally the best choice. It's modern, easy to read, and ATS-friendly.
