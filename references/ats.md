# ATS Optimization Guide

## What is ATS?

An Applicant Tracking System (ATS) is software companies use to manage recruitment. When you submit a resume online, the ATS scans it for keywords and ranks it before a human ever sees it. A resume that isn't ATS-friendly may be automatically rejected regardless of quality.

## How ATS Works

1. You submit your resume through an online portal
2. The ATS scans the document for keywords and phrases from the job description
3. Your resume is ranked based on keyword match
4. Only the top-ranked resumes are forwarded to recruiters

## Optimization Rules

### Exact Keyword Matching
ATS systems are literal — they match exact strings, not concepts.
- If the job says "React.js", write "React.js" — not "Re-act", "ReactJS", or "react"
- If the job says "frontend", write "frontend" — not "front-end" or "FrontEnd" or "front end"
- Match the exact casing and spelling from the job description

### Standard Section Headings
ATS parsers look for standard headings. Creative headings confuse them.
- Use: Summary, Experience, Work Experience, Education, Skills, Projects
- Don't use: "My Journey", "What I've Done", "Toolbox", "Where I've Learned"

### Clean Formatting
- No tables, columns, or text boxes — these break ATS parsing
- No headers or footers — ATS may skip them entirely
- No images or graphics for important information
- Use standard bullet points (•), not custom symbols
- Save as .docx or .pdf (check which the ATS prefers)
- Use standard fonts (Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman)

### Keyword Integration
Don't just stuff keywords — integrate them naturally:
- In your summary: mention key technologies and role title
- In work experience bullets: use the same terminology as the job description
- In skills section: list exact skill names from the posting
- Don't copy-paste the job description into your resume — some ATS systems detect this

### Test Your Resume
Before submitting, test if ATS can read your resume:
- Upload it to free online ATS scanners
- Check that text extracts cleanly
- Verify section headings are recognized
- Confirm keywords are detected

## Common ATS Failures

1. **Keyword mismatch** — Job says "Python" but resume says "python" (case-sensitive ATS)
2. **Creative formatting** — Two-column layouts, infographics, tables that break text extraction
3. **Wrong file format** — Some ATS can't parse certain PDF versions
4. **Text in images** — Logo, charts, or other graphics containing text
5. **Missing keywords** — Not including terms from the job description
6. **Non-standard headings** — "My Toolbox" instead of "Skills"
7. **AI-generated text** — Some ATS systems flag patterns typical of AI-written content
