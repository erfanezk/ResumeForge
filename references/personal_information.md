# Personal Information Deep Dive

## What to Include

### Your Job Title
First thing a recruiter sees. Use the title you're applying for or one that reflects your skills. "Software Engineer" or "Full Stack Developer" for a software role.

### Full Name
Legal name, no nicknames. Keep it formal — this is a professional document.

### Phone Number
Personal mobile only. Never include your work number — unprofessional and risky if your employer doesn't know you're job hunting.

### Email Address
Professional email that includes your name. Avoid quirky usernames like `coolguy123@whatever.com`. Create a new email for applications if needed.

### Location
City and state (or country) only. No full address — helps recruiters know your general location without privacy risks.

### LinkedIn Profile
Include the URL if you have one. Make sure it's customized and up to date.

### GitHub, Portfolio, or Relevant Links
For creative or tech fields, linking to a portfolio, GitHub, or personal website is valuable. Must be relevant to the job.

## What to Leave Out

- **Social Security Number** — never include, security risk
- **Date of Birth, Gender, Marital Status** — unnecessary, can lead to bias
- **Photo** — unless acting/modeling, leave it off
- **Nationality or Place of Birth** — skip unless specifically required (government roles)

## Formatting Rules

- ✅ Country name first, then city name
- ✅ Use hyperlinks for contact info
- ✅ Must include LinkedIn, and maybe GitHub or alternatives
- ✅ Write out URLs (not just hyperlinks) so they work when printed
- ✅ A good photo (professional clothes/suit) is better than no photo, and no photo is better than a bad photo
- ❌ Don't share age, religion, political views, or exact address
- ❌ Don't use unprofessional language or tone
