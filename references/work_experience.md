# Work Experience Deep Dive

## Why It Matters

The work experience section is the most important part of your resume. It's where you showcase career progression, skills, and achievements. Recruiters spend the most time here.

## Key Guidelines

### Relevancy is Key

Focus on experiences directly relevant to the target job. If a past job isn't related, either leave it out or minimize its details. Tailor your work experience to match the job description.

### Chronological Order

List work experience in reverse chronological order, starting with the most recent job. This format is preferred by most recruiters.

### Include Essential Details

For each job, provide:
- **Job Title**: Clearly state your position
- **Company Name**: Full name of the company
- **Location**: City and state
- **Dates of Employment**: Start and end dates in MM/YYYY format

### Describe Role and Achievements

Under each job, describe main responsibilities and key achievements. Focus on what you accomplished, not just duties. Quantify achievements where possible.

### Address Career Gaps

If you have gaps in employment history, be prepared to address them. Consider using a functional or combination resume format if you have significant gaps or are changing careers.

### How Far Back to Go

Generally cover the last 10-15 years. For senior positions, you may need to go further, but always prioritize relevancy over chronology. Don't list every job you've ever had.

### Bullet Point Count

Write minimum 2 bullet points, maximum 6-7 per job. If you have more than 6-7, consider merging them.

### Overlapping Dates

Jobs shouldn't overlap with each other or other experiences (except work + education overlap is allowed).
