# Publications & Conferences Deep Dive

## Why They Matter

Essential if you're in a field where research, thought leadership, or subject matter expertise is valued — academia, scientific fields, tech, healthcare. They highlight contributions and ability to communicate knowledge.

## Where to List

### Dedicated Section
Create a "Publications" or "Publications & Conferences" section after education or work experience. Can have multiple bullet points per entry.

### Within Experience Section
Include key publications directly within work experience entries if tied to a specific role. One or few bullet points per entry.

### Education Section
For recent graduates with academic publications. Appropriate if directly related to academic work (thesis, dissertation).

## How to Format

### Publications
- **Title of the Work**: Full title
- **Where Published**: Journal, book, or website
- **Date of Publication**: Month and year
- **Additional Details**: Co-authors, impact factors, page numbers

**Example:**
Doe, J., & Smith, A. (2023). The Future of AI in Healthcare. _Journal of Medical Informatics_, 12(3), 45-60.

### Conferences
- **Your Role**: Presenter, panelist, keynote speaker
- **Title of Presentation**: Full title
- **Conference Name**: Full name
- **Location and Date**: Where and when
- **Additional Details**: Conference proceedings if published

**Example:**
Presenter, "AI in Modern Diagnostics," HealthTech Conference, San Francisco, CA, March 2023.

## Rules

- ✅ Only list publications/conferences relevant to the job
- ✅ Include title, publication name, date, and your role
- ✅ Include links or citations to verify validity
- ✅ Use consistent citation style (APA, etc.)
- ✅ Reverse chronological order
- ❌ Don't include irrelevant publications (personal blog posts, unrelated articles)
- ❌ Don't include unfinalized publications
- ❌ Don't include outdated publications no longer relevant
