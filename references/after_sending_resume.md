# After Sending Your Resume

## Reach Back

A proactive approach to job searching that involves following up with recruiters or hiring managers after submitting an application. Especially useful if you haven't heard back, or if your application may not have been considered due to ATS screening.

By reaching back, you demonstrate interest in the job and willingness to go the extra mile. This helps you stand out and increases your chances of being considered.

When reaching back, be professional and courteous:
- Thank them for considering your application
- Express enthusiasm for the job
- Highlight relevant skills or experience you may not have included initially

If rejected, use it as an opportunity to ask for feedback and advice on how to improve for future opportunities. This helps identify areas to strengthen in your resume or cover letter.

## Thank You Letter

A way of expressing gratitude after an interview or for support received. Simple yet effective — a well-written thank you letter leaves a lasting positive impression.

**Structure:**
- **First paragraph**: Thank the person for their help or support and express how much it means to you
- **Second paragraph**: Elaborate on the impact their assistance has had and how it made a difference
- **Final paragraph**: Express gratitude again and let them know their help won't be forgotten
