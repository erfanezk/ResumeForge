# The Targeted Approach vs. Shotgun Approach

## What is the Shotgun Approach?

Sending your resume to as many job openings as possible, hoping something sticks. Sounds logical, but it usually backfires.

## Why the Shotgun Approach Fails

1. **Generic Applications Get Ignored**: Hiring managers spot generic resumes instantly. If your application isn't tailored, it goes in the trash. Companies want to see genuine interest in THEIR role.
2. **Wasted Effort**: 100 applications with no results is demoralizing. Focus on fewer applications with more effort each.
3. **You Lose Focus**: Applying for anything and everything makes you seem unsure about what you want. Employers want candidates who know what they're after.

## The Better Alternative: Targeted Approach

### Quality Over Quantity
Focus on roles that truly match your skills and interests. Spend time tailoring your resume and cover letter for each application. Make it clear why you're a perfect fit for THAT job.

### Research and Network
Learn about the companies you're applying to. Connect with current employees or reach out to the hiring manager. This extra effort shows dedication and sets you apart.

### Highlight Relevant Skills
Customize your resume to highlight the skills and experiences most relevant to the job. Makes it easier for recruiters to see why you're a strong candidate.

## Tips

- **Use a Spreadsheet**: Track jobs you apply to — company name, job title, date applied, and other relevant details. Stay organized and follow up effectively.
- **Understand Job Requirements**: The key to success. Tailor your resume to match requirements. Put yourself in the hiring manager's shoes — what are they looking for? Research the company's product, stack, and culture.

## Final Thoughts

Applying to fewer jobs with more focus and customization is far more effective than the shotgun approach. It takes more time, but the chances of landing an interview — and ultimately a job — are much higher. It's not about applying to every job; it's about applying to the right jobs with the right application.
