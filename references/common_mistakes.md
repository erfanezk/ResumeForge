# Common Mistakes Deep Dive

The 10 most common resume mistakes. If your resume has even one of these, fixing it can significantly increase your interview chances.

## 1. Complex Language

Don't use words that are too hard to understand and follow.

- ❌ "Executed a multitude of tasks pertaining to the optimization of manifold software paradigms."
- ✅ "Improved software performance by 20%"

## 2. Buzzword Overload

Don't use buzzwords in a way that seems unnatural and excessive.

- ❌ "Leveraged agile methodologies in a cross-functional team to ideate scalable efficient solutions"
- ✅ "Leveraged Agile practices to improve team collaboration"

## 3. Unverifiable Metrics

Sometimes people rate their resumes with AI tools, resulting in many invalid numbers. In the interview you're responsible for every number and percentage in your resume — don't overdo it.

- ❌ "Increased code performance by 200%" (without context or evidence)
- ✅ "Optimized SQL queries by creating a caching layer, reducing load time by 50%, approved by A/B testing for the release candidate"

## 4. Unrelated Achievements

The hiring manager doesn't care about personal life achievements unrelated to the job. Keep your resume relevant to the position.

- ❌ "Increase sales and turnover with business strategy" (you're a developer applying for a developer role)
- ✅ "Designed and implemented a caching solution that improved system latency"

## 5. Invalid Technical Statements

Make sure claims in your resume are technically correct. Saying you "optimized code by following clean code principles" is not technically correct — those are two different matters.

- ❌ "Optimized code by following clean code principles"
- ✅ "Refactored legacy code to improve readability and maintainability"

## 6. Not ATS-Friendly

If your resume isn't ATS-friendly, even if it's the best in the world, it won't be considered. Read the ATS section and apply the recommended solutions.

- ❌ Applying for "React Frontend Position" but your CV says "front-end" and React isn't mentioned
- ✅ Understanding how ATS works and writing your resume to work with it, not against it

## 7. Poorly Written Skills

People often mention skills they don't really know. Writing your skills section carefully increases chances of getting both the interview and the job offer.

- ❌ (Python Backend Position): "Expert in Java, C++, Python, Machine Learning, MariaDB, PostgreSQL, MySQL, FastAPI, Django, SQL, Spring Boot, Docker"
- ✅ (Python Backend Position): "Python, Docker, Django, FastAPI, SQL"

## 8. Generic Summary

Some people overdo the About Me section, or make it too generic — more like "About Any Python Developer" rather than about being YOU.

- ❌ Generic summary that could apply to any developer with your job title
- ✅ Specific, personal summary backed by evidence in your resume

## 9. No Bullet Points

If your resume doesn't have bullet points, you're almost certainly getting rejected. This is resume 101.

- ❌ Writing paragraphs that make it hard to quickly identify key information
- ✅ Clear, structured bullet points for each role

## 10. Bad/Invalid Structure

Some people don't follow general resume conventions, which results in rejection. Use a standard format — use a resume builder website if needed.
