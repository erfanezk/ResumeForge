# Education Section Deep Dive

## Keep It Relevant

Your education should reflect what's most relevant to the job. If you have a degree, no need to mention high school unless it's the highest level completed. For specialized fields, highlight specific coursework, projects, or certifications matching the job.

## Structure It Clearly

Start with your highest degree and work backward:
- Degree type
- Major/field of study
- Institution name
- Location
- Graduation date (or expected date if still studying)

For ongoing education: "In progress" or "Expected completion" with month and year.

### Example

**Bachelor of Science in Computer Science**
University of XYZ, City, State
Graduated: May 2023
GPA: 3.8/4.0, Dean's List, Relevant Coursework: Data Structures, AI, Algorithms

## Highlight Achievements and Honors

Don't just list the degree — showcase what you achieved. Graduated with honors, Dean's List, scholarships — include them. Quantifiable achievements add weight, especially if you lack work experience.

## Tailor It to the Job

If applying for a role requiring specific knowledge, highlight relevant coursework or projects. If a PhD is required, mention it even in your summary.

## Be Honest

Be 100% about your education. Employers can verify credentials easily. If you didn't finish a degree, list completed credits without explaining why — that's an interview conversation, not a resume one.

## Placement

If fresh out of school or education is a key qualifier, put it near the top. For experienced professionals, place it below work history.

## Rules

- ✅ Most recent degree or certification first
- ✅ Include institution name, degree/certification, major/field, graduation date
- ✅ Include GPA if strong
- ❌ Don't mention high school unless it's your highest level of education
