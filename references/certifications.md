# Certifications & Trainings Deep Dive

## Why They Matter

Certifications validate your skills, show commitment to professional growth, and help you stand out. For roles requiring specific qualifications, they can be the difference between getting an interview or not.

## Where to List Them

- **Dedicated Section**: Create a "Certifications" or "Certifications & Training" section below education or experience. Best if you have multiple to showcase.
- **Resume Summary**: If a certification is crucial for the job, mention it in your summary for immediate visibility.
- **Contact Information**: For roles requiring a specific cert (PMP, CPA, RN), list it next to your name. Especially effective if the job posting emphasizes it.
- **Education Section**: If obtained as part of formal education, or if relevant but not the main focus.

## How to Format

Each listing should include:
- **Full Certification Title**: Avoid abbreviations unless industry-standard
- **Issuing Organization**: Full name of the certifying body
- **Date Earned/Expected**: When you obtained it, or expected completion date
- **Expiration Date**: If the certification needs renewal
- **Details (optional)**: Short description of skills or knowledge gained

### Example

**Certifications**
- AWS Certified Solutions Architect – Professional, Amazon Web Services (AWS), Earned: March 2023
- Certified Project Management Professional (PMP), Project Management Institute (PMI), Earned: June 2021, Expires: June 2024

## Rules

- ✅ Only include certifications relevant to the job
- ✅ Reverse-chronological order, most recent first
- ✅ Include name, issuing organization, date obtained
- ✅ Hyperlink if it's a digital certificate
- ❌ Don't include expired or incomplete certifications
- ❌ Don't make up or exaggerate certifications
- ❌ Don't provide incomplete or inaccurate info — easily verified, hurts credibility
