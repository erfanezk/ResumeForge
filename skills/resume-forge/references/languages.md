# Languages Deep Dive

Language skills give you a competitive edge in global companies and multinational teams. List only what you can actually use professionally.

## Why It Matters

- Demonstrates ability to work in international environments
- Essential for companies with global teams or customers
- Shows cultural awareness and adaptability
- Can be a hard requirement for certain roles (localization, translation, international business)

**Rule:** Your resume itself is a writing sample. Make sure it's error-free — that's your English proficiency proof.

## Where to List Languages

| Situation | Placement | Format |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Relevant to the role | Skills section or dedicated "Languages" section | "Spanish — Professional Working Proficiency" |
| Multiple languages | Dedicated "Languages" section after experience | Full section with proficiency levels |
| Required for the job | Mention in summary + skills + languages section | Reinforce across sections |
| Not relevant at all | Don't list | Skip unless you're explicitly multilingual |

## Standard Proficiency Levels

Use these recognized ILR or CEFR-based levels — **never** star ratings:

| Level | Description | When to Use |
|-------|-------------|------------|
| Native or Bilingual Proficiency | Like a native speaker | Mother tongue or grew up speaking |
| Full Professional Proficiency | Professional working proficiency in all contexts | Working fluently in professional settings |
| Professional Working Proficiency | Can participate effectively in most professional conversations | Working knowledge — good for business |
| Limited Working Proficiency | Can handle routine social situations and basic work tasks | Survival level — avoid unless relevant |
| Elementary Proficiency | Basic greetings and simple phrases | Don't list — too minimal |

**Note:** Avoid visual ratings (stars, bars, percentage) — they're subjective and not ATS-friendly.

## How to Format

### Template

```
[Language] — [Proficiency Level]
[Language] — [Proficiency Level] | [Certification if applicable]
```

### Formatting Examples

```
Languages

English — Native or Bilingual Proficiency
Spanish — Full Professional Proficiency (DELE B2 Certified)
Mandarin — Professional Working Proficiency
```

## Certifications as Proof

| Certification | What It Measures |
|--------------|-----------------|
| TOEFL / IELTS | English proficiency for non-native speakers |
| DELF / DALF | French (Alliance Française) |
| DELE | Spanish (Instituto Cervantes) |
| HSK | Mandarin Chinese |
| Goethe-Zertifikat | German |

**Format with cert:**
```
Spanish — Full Professional Proficiency (DELE C1 Certified, 2022)
```

## Before/After Transformations

| Before (Weak) | After (Strong) | Why Better |
|--------------|----------------|------------|
| "Speaks Spanish pretty well" | "Spanish — Professional Working Proficiency" | Standard language, not casual |
| "⭐⭐⭐⭐ French, ⭐⭐ German" | "French — Full Professional Proficiency, German — Professional Working Proficiency" | Standard levels, no star ratings |
| "Knows some Python, English" (under skills section, mixed) | Separate languages section: "English — Native, Spanish — Professional Working" | Correct section, standard format |
| "Fluent in 3 languages" | "English — Native, Mandarin — Full Professional, French — Professional Working" | Specific, honest, professional |

## How Many to List

| Situation | How Many |
|-----------|---------|
| Relevant to the job | All you're actually proficient in |
| Not relevant | Only your best 2-3 (your native + one or two strong ones) |
| Job requires multilingual staff | List all relevant languages with honest proficiency |

**Rule:** If you list it, be prepared to use it in an interview. If you can't hold a conversation in that language, don't list it.

## Which to List

| Include | Exclude |
|---------|--------|
| Languages you're professionally proficient in | Languages you can't use in a work setting |
| Native or bilingual proficiency | Elementary proficiency — too minimal |
| Languages required or relevant to the job | Star ratings, bars, or percentage scales |
| Certifications backing proficiency claims | Basic survival phrases |

## Rules

- ✅ Use standard proficiency labels (Native, Full Professional, Professional Working)
- ✅ List languages you're proficient in or that relate to the job
- ✅ Backup claims with certification if the job requires proof
- ✅ Only include languages you can actually use in a professional setting
- ❌ Don't list languages you're not proficient in (unless native/bilingual)
- ❌ Don't use star ratings, percentage bars, or other subjective scales
- ❌ Don't list basic survival phrases — they look unprofessional
- ❌ Don't overstate proficiency — you may be tested in the interview
