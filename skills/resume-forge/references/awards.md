# Awards Deep Dive

Awards provide concrete proof of excellence and help you stand out. One strong award is worth more than five minor ones.

## Why They Matter

- Provide **third-party validation** of your skills and work ethic
- Differentiate you from candidates with similar experience
- Demonstrate sustained excellence, not just task completion
- Show recognition beyond your immediate team

**Rule:** List awards where they provide the most context. An award under the job it was earned at shows both the achievement and the employer who recognized it.

## Where to List

| Context | Where to List | Example |
|---------|--------------|---------|
| Academic awards | Education section | Dean's List, scholarship recipient |
| Work performance awards | Work Experience section, under the job | Employee of the Year, Innovation Award |
| Multiple prestigious awards | Dedicated "Awards" or "Honors" section | Hackathon winner, industry recognition |
| Industry/professional awards | After work experience or dedicated section | AWS Community Hero, OSCON Speaker Award |

## What to Include Per Award

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Name of the Award | The official award title | "Excellence in Engineering Award" |
| Awarding Body | Full name of the organization | "Acme Corp Leadership Team" |
| Date Received | Month and year | "March 2023" |
| Context | Brief explanation of why you received it | "Selected from 200+ engineers for technical leadership" |

## How to Format

### Template

```
[AWARD NAME] — [Awarding Body] | [Month Year]
[Context — why you received it and what it says about you]
```

### Example

```
Excellence in Engineering Award — Acme Corp | March 2023
Selected from 200+ engineers for leading a migration that reduced
infrastructure costs by 30%.
```

## Before/After Transformations

| Before (Weak) | After (Strong) | Why Better |
|--------------|----------------|------------|
| "Won an award at work" | "Employee of the Quarter, Acme Corp, Q3 2023 — top 5% performer out of 150 engineers" | Specific, quantifiable, context |
| "Got a scholarship" | "Dean's List Scholar, University of XYZ, 2020-2023 — awarded to top 5% of engineering students" | Scope and selectivity clear |
| "Best team player award" | "Team Innovation Award, TechConf 2023 — selected from 50 teams for an open-source contribution" | Specific event and selection criteria |
| "Received recognition" | "Spot Bonus Award, Stripe, June 2022 — for designing a fraud detection system that prevented $150K in monthly losses" | Specific achievement, monetary context |

## How Many Awards to List

| Experience Level | Recommended Awards |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Entry-level (0-2 years) | 1-3 (academic or early work awards) |
| Mid-level (2-5 years) | 2-4 (work performance awards) |
| Senior (5-10 years) | 1-3 (impactful, role-relevant only) |
| Staff / Principal | 1-2 (prestigious industry recognition) |

**Rule:** Quality over quantity. A single award with full context beats 5 vague mentions.

## Which Awards to List

| Include | Exclude |
|---------|--------|
| Awards relevant to your field | Irrelevant or trivial awards (e.g., "Best Costume" for a developer role) |
| Official awards recognized widely | Participation trophies — only actual wins or recognitions |
| Recent awards or major industry awards | Awards from decades ago (unless particularly prestigious) |
| Awards with context — why you received them | Generic mentions with no context |
| Third-party validation of your skills | Self-awarded "recognition" without validation |

## Rules

- ✅ Only include awards relevant to your field
- ✅ Include award name, awarding organization, and date
- ✅ Only list official awards accepted or recognized widely
- ✅ Provide context — why you received it and what it says about you
- ✅ Prioritize recent awards over old ones unless particularly prestigious
- ❌ Don't include irrelevant or trivial awards
- ❌ Don't list awards from decades ago unless they're major industry recognitions
- ❌ Don't list participation trophies — only actual wins or recognitions
