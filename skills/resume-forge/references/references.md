# References Deep Dive

References back up your claims with people who can speak to your work. Most modern resumes don't include them directly — but you must be prepared to provide them when asked.

## Why They Matter

- Provide third-party validation of your skills and work ethic
- Give recruiters confidence in hiring you
- Fill gaps where your resume can't tell the full story
- Add credibility beyond what you can claim yourself

**Rule:** Don't include references on your resume. Prepare a separate document to provide when requested.

## When to Include References

| Situation | What to Do |
|-----------|-----------|
| Job posting explicitly requests them | Include a separate references page |
| Entry-level with limited work history | References can bridge the credibility gap |
| Career change with transferable skills | References from different fields can validate soft skills |
| Senior role with notable referees | References from industry leaders add significant weight |

**In all cases:** Provide on a separate page — not on the resume itself.

## What to Include Per Reference

| Field | Description | Example |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Full name | Legal name | "John Smith" |
| Job title | Their current or most recent title | "Engineering Manager" |
| Company | Full company name | "Acme Corp" |
| Relationship | How you know them | "Former manager, TechCorp 2020-2023" |
| Phone | Direct line (not general office) | "+1 (555) 987-6543" |
| Email | Professional email | "john.smith@acmecorp.com" |
| Best way to contact | Their preferred method | "Email preferred" |

## How to Format

### Template

```
[Reference Name]
[Job Title], [Company Name]
[Relationship — how you worked together]
Phone: [number] | Email: [email]
Preferred contact: [email/phone]
```

### Example Reference

```
Jane Anderson
Senior Engineering Manager, Acme Corp
Former direct manager at TechCorp, 2020-2023 — managed 12 engineers,
oversaw platform migration project.
Phone: +1 (555) 987-6543 | Email: jane.anderson@acmecorp.com
Preferred contact: Email
```

## Who to Choose

| Good Reference | Why |
|----------------|-----|
| Direct manager (recent) | Can speak to your performance and leadership |
| Senior engineer (collaborated with) | Validates technical skills |
| Skip-level manager | Broader view of your impact across teams |
| Client or external stakeholder | Proves external-facing soft skills |
| Peer who can speak to collaboration | Shows team dynamics and culture fit |

**Avoid:**
- Friends or family (not professional)
- Former managers with poor relationships
- Colleagues who can't speak to your technical skills
- References who haven't heard from you in years

## How Many to Include

| Situation | How Many |
|-----------|---------|
| Standard request | 3-4 references |
| Executive/senior role | 3-5 references |
| Entry-level | 3 (mix of academic and work references) |

**Rule:** Choose references who can speak to different strengths. Don't list 3 engineering managers who will say the same thing.

## Best Practices

| Practice | Why It Matters |
|----------|---------------|
| Ask permission first | Always — never share contact info without asking |
| Keep references updated | Remove old refs, add new ones as you progress |
| Brief your references | Tell them what role you're applying for and your key selling points |
| Provide context before the interview | Send your resume and the job description to your refs |
| Choose refs relevant to the role | Leadership role → include manager; technical role → include engineers |
| Keep the format consistent | Match your resume's style on the separate references page |

## The "References Available Upon Request" Line

| Before (Outdated) | After (Skip It) |
|------------------|-----------------|
| "References available upon request" | Don't include it — every employer assumes you have references |

**Rule:** This line is outdated and wastes a line on your resume. If they want references, they'll ask.

## Formatting the References Page

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Font and sizes | Match your resume exactly |
| Header | Your name + contact info — same as resume |
| Margins | Same as resume |
| Layout | Clean, professional, single-column |
| File name | `YourName_References.pdf` |

**Template:**
```
John Smith — References
Backend Developer | +1 (555) 123-4567 | john.smith@gmail.com

[Reference 1]

[Reference 2]

[Reference 3]
```

## Rules

- ✅ Provide references on a separate page — not on the resume
- ✅ Ask permission before listing anyone
- ✅ Keep references updated — stale info reflects poorly on you
- ✅ Brief your references before the interview
- ✅ Choose 3-4 people who can speak to different strengths
- ✅ Match the references page format to your resume
- ❌ Don't include references on the resume itself
- ❌ Don't list friends or family
- ❌ Don't include the "References available upon request" line
- ❌ Don't list references who can't speak to your professional skills
- ❌ Don't list former managers with whom you had poor relationships
