# Personal Information Deep Dive

Your contact information is how recruiters reach you. It must be professional, complete, and impossible to ignore.

## Why It Matters

- First impression on every recruiter who sees your resume
- How recruiters schedule interviews and follow up
- Sets the professional tone before any content is read
- Missing or incorrect info = lost opportunity

**Rule:** Recruiters should be able to reach you in 3 seconds. If they can't, they move on.

## Required Information

| Field | What to Include | Example |
|-------|----------------|---------|
| Full name | Legal name — no nicknames or casual names | "John Smith" (not "Johnny" or "J. Smith") |
| Job title | The role you're applying for or your professional title | "Backend Developer" or "Senior Software Engineer" |
| Phone | Personal mobile only — never your work number | "+1 (555) 123-4567" |
| Email | Professional email with your name | "john.smith@gmail.com" (not "coolguy123@...") |
| Location | City, State/Region, Country | "San Francisco, CA, USA" or "Berlin, Germany" |
| LinkedIn | Full URL to your personalized profile | "linkedin.com/in/johnsmithdev" |
| GitHub/Portfolio | Only if relevant to the role | "github.com/johnsmithdev" |

## How to Format

### Template

```
[Your Full Name]
[Job Title]
[Phone] | [Email] | [Location]
[LinkedIn URL] | [GitHub/Portfolio URL]
```

### Example Header

```
John Smith
Backend Developer
+1 (555) 123-4567 | john.smith@gmail.com | San Francisco, CA, USA
linkedin.com/in/johnsmithdev | github.com/johnsmithdev
```

## Optional But Recommended

| Field | When to Include | Format |
|-------|----------------|--------|
| Website/Portfolio | If you have a professional site | "johnsmith.dev" |
| Stack Overflow | If you have a strong profile | "stackoverflow.com/users/12345/johnsmith" |
| Twitter/X | If you post professional content | "@johnsmithdev" |

## What to Leave Out

| Field | Why to Exclude |
|-------|---------------|
| Social Security Number | Extreme security risk — identity theft |
| Date of Birth | Leads to age discrimination |
| Gender / Marital Status | Leads to bias, not relevant to job performance |
| Photo | Unless specifically required (acting, modeling) — ATS doesn't process it |
| Full home address | Privacy risk, unnecessary — city/state is enough |
| Nationality / Place of Birth | Unnecessary unless government role requires it |
| Age | "22-year-old developer" can trigger unconscious bias |
| Religion / Political views | Completely irrelevant to job performance |
| Family status | Not a hiring factor — illegal to consider in many countries |

**Note:** In some countries (EU, parts of Asia), you may be legally required to include certain information (nationality, age). Research the norms for your target market.

## Photo Rules

| Situation | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------------|
| Most roles (US, UK, Canada) | No photo — ATS doesn't process it, recruiters may ignore it |
| European companies | Some expect photos — research the company norm |
| Creative roles (modeling, acting) | Photo required — use a professional headshot |
| Tech industry (most markets) | No photo — focus on GitHub and LinkedIn instead |

**General rule:** When in doubt, leave out the photo. Your skills and experience are what get you the interview.

## Formatting Rules

- ✅ Country first, then city: "Germany — Berlin" not "Berlin, Germany" (for international)
- ✅ Use hyperlinks for contact information — makes it easy to click/call
- ✅ Write out URLs fully so they work when printed
- ✅ LinkedIn must be personalized, not the default random ID
- ✅ Keep formatting clean — recruiters should be able to reach you in 3 seconds
- ✅ Update LinkedIn before applying — ensure the URL is customized and profile is current
- ❌ Don't use unprofessional email addresses (coolguy123, partyhard@...)
- ❌ Don't use your work phone — unprofessional if your employer doesn't know you're job searching
- ❌ Don't include anything that could trigger bias (age, gender, religion, photo)
- ❌ Don't include overly personal information or hobbies

## Rules

- ✅ Include your legal name, job title, phone, email, and location
- ✅ Include LinkedIn (personalized URL) and GitHub/Portfolio if relevant
- ✅ Use professional email addresses with your name
- ✅ Write out URLs fully for print compatibility
- ✅ Keep formatting clean and scannable
- ✅ Country first, then city for international addresses
- ❌ Don't use nicknames or informal names
- ❌ Don't use unprofessional email addresses
- ❌ Don't include full home address — city and country is enough
- ❌ Don't include photos unless specifically required for the role
- ❌ Don't include anything that could lead to bias (age, religion, gender, marital status)
