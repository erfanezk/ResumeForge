# The Targeted Approach vs. Shotgun Approach

Two strategies for job applications. One works. One doesn't.

## Why It Matters

- Shotgun approach: 100 applications, <5% response rate, exhausted and demoralized
- Targeted approach: 15 applications, 15-30% response rate, more interviews, better offers
- Same number of interviews — completely different effort-to-result ratio

**Rule:** It's not about applying to every job — it's about applying to the right jobs with the right application.

## The Shotgun Approach

Sending your resume to every job opening you find, hoping something sticks.

### Why It Fails

| Problem | Why It Happens |
|---------|---------------|
| Generic applications get ignored | Hiring managers spot one-size-fits-all resumes immediately |
| Wasted effort | 100 applications with no results is demoralizing |
| You lose focus | Applying for everything makes you seem unsure about your goals |
| ATS filters catch generic content | Keywords don't match specific roles |
| Recruiter time is limited | They spend 6 seconds on a resume — generic = discarded |

**Data point:** Studies show the average job search sends 100-200 applications with a <5% response rate. The shotgun approach has a high floor but a low ceiling.

## The Targeted Approach

Applying to fewer jobs with significantly more effort and customization per application.

### How It Works

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| 1. Research | Learn the company — product, tech stack, culture, recent news |
| 2. Match | Align your resume keywords to the exact job description |
| 3. Customize | Write a cover letter (or summary) specific to that company and role |
| 4. Network | Connect with current employees or the hiring manager on LinkedIn |
| 5. Follow up | Reach out after 5-7 business days if no response |

### Why It Works Better

| Benefit | Impact |
|---------|--------|
| Higher response rate | 15-30% response rate for well-targeted applications |
| Stronger interview performance | You've researched the company and role deeply |
| Better cultural fit signals | Customization shows genuine interest, not just applying anywhere |
| Negotiating leverage | Multiple offers (from focused applications) beat desperation |
| Less time wasted | 10 well-targeted applications > 100 generic ones |

## How to Research a Company

| Research Area | What to Find | Where to Look |
|--------------|-------------|---------------|
| Product/Service | What they build and who uses it | Company website, recent news |
| Tech stack | What technologies they use | Job description, GitHub, engineering blog |
| Culture | Engineering practices, team size | Glassdoor, LinkedIn, company blog |
| Recent news | Funding, product launches, leadership changes | Tech news sites, company's social media |
| Interview process | How they hire, what's assessed | Glassdoor reviews, recruiter conversations |
| Pain points | Problems the team is trying to solve | Job description language, company blog |

## The Math That Changes Everything

| Approach | Applications | Response Rate | Interviews | Time Per Application |
|----------|-------------|---------------|------------|---------------------|
| Shotgun | 100 | 3% | 3 | 30 min |
| Targeted | 15 | 20% | 3 | 2 hours |

**Same number of interviews.** Massive difference in effort efficiency. With the targeted approach, you also go into each interview knowing far more about the company — which gives you an edge.

## Tracking Your Applications

| Column | What to Track |
|--------|--------------|
| Company | Full company name |
| Role | Job title |
| Date Applied | When you submitted |
| Source | Where you found it (LinkedIn, referral, company site) |
| Status | Applied, Interviewing, Rejected, Offer |
| Follow-up date | When to reach out if no response |
| Contact | Recruiter name and email |
| Notes | Anything specific to this application |

**Tip:** Set a calendar reminder to follow up 5-7 business days after applying. Most candidates never follow up — it separates you from the pack.

## Before/After Transformations

| Before (Shotgun Mind) | After (Targeted Mind) | Why Better |
|---------------------|---------------------|------------|
| "I'll apply to everything and see what sticks" | "I'll focus on 15 companies where I'm genuinely excited about the role" | Quality over quantity |
| "Same resume for every application" | "Resume keywords matched to each job description" | ATS passes, recruiter notices |
| "No research, just apply" | "I know their tech stack, product, and culture before applying" | Shows genuine interest |
| "Apply and forget" | "Follow up 5-7 days after applying" | Separates you from 90% of candidates |
| "Track nothing" | "Spreadsheet with all applications, status, and follow-ups" | Stay organized, follow up effectively |

## Common Mistakes

| Mistake | Why It Fails | Fix |
|---------|-------------|-----|
| Applying to anything that matches a keyword | ATS matches, but recruiter sees generic | Only apply to roles you genuinely want |
| No follow-up | Most candidates never follow up | Set reminders to follow up after 5-7 days |
| No tracking | Lost in the noise | Use a spreadsheet to track every application |
| Copy-pasting job description | ATS flags keyword stuffing | Inject keywords naturally into your bullets |
| Applying to 200+ roles | Exhaustion, low-quality applications | Focus on 15-20 strong applications instead |

## Rules

- ✅ Research the company before applying — know their product, tech stack, and culture
- ✅ Match your resume keywords to the exact job description
- ✅ Customize your summary per application
- ✅ Follow up 5-7 business days after submitting with no response
- ✅ Track every application in a spreadsheet
- ✅ Apply to fewer jobs — quality over quantity
- ❌ Don't send the same generic resume to every role
- ❌ Don't apply to jobs you wouldn't actually take
- ❌ Don't skip follow-ups — most candidates never do
- ❌ Don't apply to 100+ roles with no tracking system
- ❌ Don't keyword stuff — integrate terms naturally
