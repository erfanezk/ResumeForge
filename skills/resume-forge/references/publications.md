# Publications Deep Dive

Publications show you contribute to your field's body of knowledge. They're most valuable in academia, research, and technical leadership roles.

## Why They Matter

- Demonstrates subject matter expertise beyond day-to-day work
- Signals ability to communicate complex ideas clearly
- Shows commitment to advancing the field
- Provides third-party validation of your thinking

**Rule:** Only list publications you can defend in an interview. If a reviewer asks about your paper, you must be able to explain every claim.

## Where to List

| Context | Placement | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Multiple publications | Dedicated "Publications" or "Publications & Conferences" section | After Education or Experience |
| Single highly relevant publication | Work Experience section as bullet under relevant role | Tied to a specific job |
| Academic publication (recent grad) | Education section under the degree | Thesis or dissertation |
| Conference talk | Dedicated section or within Experience | Tied to specific role |

## Publication Types

### Journal Articles

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Title of the Work | Full title — don't abbreviate |
| Publication Name | Journal name + volume/issue/pages |
| Date | Month and year of publication |
| Co-authors | If applicable |
| Impact factor | Optional but adds credibility |
| Link | DOI or accessible version |

**Format:**
```
[Author(s)]. ([Year]). [Title]. [Journal Name], [Volume]([Issue]), [Pages].
DOI: [link]
```

**Example:**
```
Smith, J., & Doe, A. (2023). Real-Time Anomaly Detection in Streaming Data.
IEEE Transactions on Big Data, 9(2), 145-162.
DOI: 10.1109/TBD.2023.3012456
```

### Conference Papers

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Title of Presentation | Full title |
| Conference Name | Full name + location |
| Date | Month and year |
| Your Role | Presenter, Co-author, Panelist |
| Proceedings | If published in conference proceedings |

**Format:**
```
[Author(s)]. ([Year]). [Title]. In [Conference Name], [Location].
```

**Example:**
```
Smith, J. (2023). Scaling Microservices for High-Throughput Payment Systems.
In Proceedings of IEEE ICASSP 2023, Vancouver, BC, Canada.
```

### Conference Talks and Presentations

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| Title | Full title of your talk |
| Conference Name | Full name |
| Location and Date | Where and when |
| Your Role | Keynote, Presenter, Panelist, Workshop lead |
| Audience size | If notable — adds credibility |

**Format:**
```
[Role]: "[Talk Title]" — [Conference Name], [Location], [Month Year]
```

**Example:**
```
Presenter: "Building Resilient Payment Systems at Scale" — PyCon 2023,
Salt Lake City, UT, April 2023 (audience: 800+ engineers)
```

## Rules

- ✅ Only list publications directly relevant to your field or target job
- ✅ Include title, publication name, date, and your role
- ✅ Use consistent citation style (APA, IEEE, etc.)
- ✅ Include links or DOIs for verification
- ✅ Reverse chronological order — most recent first
- ✅ List co-authors if significant (shows collaboration)
- ❌ Don't list personal blog posts (unless major industry publication)
- ❌ Don't list publications that are still in progress or under review
- ❌ Don't list outdated publications no longer relevant to your career
- ❌ Don't include self-published content without significant reach

## How Many to List

| Experience Level | How Many |
|-----------------|---------|
| Entry-level (0-2 years) | 1-3 (thesis, conference papers, class projects) |
| Mid-level (2-5 years) | 2-5 (work-related publications or conference talks) |
| Senior (5-10 years) | 2-4 (major publications or keynote talks) |
| Staff / Principal | 2-3 (influential, widely cited publications) |

**Rule:** 2-3 strong, relevant publications beat 10 minor ones. Quality and relevance over quantity.
