# Resume Structure Deep Dive

Structure determines whether your resume gets read or ignored. Both content structure and visual layout matter.

## Why It Matters

- Recruiters spend 6 seconds on first scan — structure controls what they see
- ATS systems parse based on structure — wrong structure = rejection
- Content order signals what you value — and what the recruiter should value
- Visual layout affects both human readability and ATS parsing

**Rule:** Every section should earn its place. 2 great pages beat 2 mediocre pages. Cut aggressively.

## Content Order

Standard section order for most experienced professionals:

```
1. Personal Data (header — name, title, contact)
2. Summary (2-4 lines — your elevator pitch)
3. Skills (5-8 focused skills — no ratings or descriptions)
4. Work Experience (bullets, most recent first)
5. Education (brief, most recent first)
6. Other Sections (Projects, Certifications, Awards, Languages — as relevant)
```

**Order within sections:** Reverse chronological — most recent entry on top.

### Placement Variations

| Situation | Structure Change |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Recent graduate (0-2 years) | Education above Experience, Projects section prominent |
| Career changer | Summary emphasizes transferable skills, Experience reordered by relevance |
| Senior/Staff (10+ years) | Work Experience first, Education minimal, Leadership bullets |
| Academia/research | Education and Publications sections above Work Experience |

## Visual Layout

### One Column (Recommended)

```
[Name — Job Title]
[Contact Info]

[Summary]
[Skills]
[Experience]
[Education]
[Additional Sections]
```

**Why:** Clear, modern, ATS-friendly, easy to scan at a glance.

### Two Column (Avoid)

```
[Name] | [Contact]     | [Summary]           |
                            [Skills]            |
[Experience]              | [Education]         |
                            [Other]              |
```

**Why:** Breaks ATS parsing, harder to scan, visually confusing.

## Page Count Rules

| Experience Level | Page Count | Rationale |
|------------------|------------|-----------|
| 0-2 years | 1 page | Limited experience — no need for 2 pages |
| 2-7 years | 1 page (preferable), 2 OK | 7 years of relevant experience fills one page well |
| 7-10 years | 1-2 pages | Balance — senior enough for 2 pages, but quality over quantity |
| 10+ years | 2 pages | Significant experience, but still cut aggressively |

## Content Structure Styles

| Style | Best For | Avoid When |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Chronological | Steady career progression, relevant work history | Job gaps, career changers |
| Functional | Career changers with transferable skills, significant gaps | Most cases — not ATS-friendly |
| Combination | Candidates with limited work history | Senior professionals |

**Rule:** Chronological is the default. Use it unless you have a specific reason not to.

## General Formatting Rules

| Element | Rule |
|---------|------|
| Name + job title | Top, large font (16-20pt name, 12-14pt title) |
| Body font | 11-12pt, Arial or Calibri |
| Headers | 14-16pt, can use a subtle color (dark blue or gray) |
| Body text | Black only — no color in bullet content |
| Date format | MM/YYYY consistently throughout |
| Line spacing | 1.0-1.15 — enough white space, not too sparse |
| Margins | 0.5-0.75 inches — enough space, not wasted |
| Bullet style | • (standard bullet) — not custom symbols |
| Word count | 475-600 words total |
| File name | `YourName_Date.pdf` — "JohnSmith_2024-01-15.pdf" |

## Section-Specific Rules

| Section | Rules |
|---------|-------|
| Summary | 2-4 lines, third person, specific + backed by resume |
| Skills | 5-8 skills, no ratings, no descriptions, category grouping optional |
| Experience | 3-5 bullets per job, action verb + what + how + result |
| Education | Brief, top degree first, GPA only if 3.5+ |
| Projects | Purpose, technologies, results — linked if applicable |
| Certifications | Full name, issuing org, date, expiration if applicable |
| Awards | Name, org, date, context for why you received it |
| Languages | Proficiency level (not stars) — see Languages Deep Dive |

## Common Structural Flags

| Flag | Problem | Fix |
|------|---------|-----|
| Two-column layout | ATS parsing fails | Switch to single-column |
| Creative section names | ATS can't read "My Journey" as Experience | Use standard: Experience, Work Experience |
| No summary | 6-second scan has no hook | Add 2-4 line summary at top |
| Too many bullets (>7 per job) | Recruiter reads nothing | Combine related bullets, keep 3-5 strongest |
| Overlapping dates | Raises questions | Resolve gaps — don't hide them |
| Inconsistent date format | Looks unprofessional | Use MM/YYYY everywhere, no exceptions |
| More than 2 pages | Gets cut by ATS or recruiter | Cut aggressively — quality over quantity |

## Before/After Transformations

| Before (Bad Structure) | After (Good Structure) | Why Better |
|-----------------------|----------------------|------------|
| Two-column layout, creative headings, no summary | Single column, standard headings, summary at top | ATS-safe, scannable |
| "My Journey" as section heading | "Work Experience" as section heading | ATS can parse it |
| 10 bullets per job | 3-5 bullets per job, merged and focused | Recruiter actually reads it |
| 3 pages for 3 years of experience | 1 page, focused | Concise, ATS-friendly |
| Education above experience (senior dev) | Experience above education | Experience is the main event |

## Rules

- ✅ Single-column layout throughout
- ✅ Standard section headings (Summary, Experience, Education, Skills)
- ✅ 3-5 bullets per job, 2-4 line summary at top
- ✅ Reverse chronological order within sections
- ✅ MM/YYYY date format consistently
- ✅ 475-600 words total, 1-2 pages depending on experience
- ✅ Bullet points (•), not paragraphs
- ✅ Cut aggressively — every line must earn its place
- ❌ Don't use two-column layouts or tables
- ❌ Don't use creative section headings (ATS can't read them)
- ❌ Don't list more than 7 bullets per job
- ❌ Don't put education above experience (unless recent grad)
- ❌ Don't use non-standard date formats
- ❌ Don't exceed 2 pages
