# Resume Templates Deep Dive

Use a professional template — don't build from scratch. Templates ensure proper formatting, ATS compatibility, and visual hierarchy without guesswork.

## Why It Matters

- Professional templates ensure ATS compatibility
- Proper visual hierarchy makes content scannable
- Saves hours of formatting work
- Ensures consistency across applications

**Rule:** The best template is invisible. Recruiters notice content — not template design. If they notice your template, you've made it too creative.

## Template Quality Checklist

| Feature | Good | Bad |
|---------|------|-----|
| Layout | Single column | Two columns, sidebar |
| Section headings | "Experience", "Education" | "My Journey", "What I've Done" |
| Contact info | Text URLs | Logo with phone number inside |
| Bullets | Standard • | Custom icons, arrows, diamonds |
| Fonts | Arial, Calibri, Times New Roman | Decorative or script fonts |
| Colors | Black text, one accent color for headers | Multiple colors, gradients |
| File format | PDF export available | Web-only (can't download PDF) |

## How to Use a Template

1. **Pick one template** — don't mix styles. Consistency builds trust.
2. **Customize content, not design** — the template handles layout, you handle words.
3. **Keep the design conservative** — your skills and experience are what get you the interview, not a flashy template.
4. **Preview before submitting** — export as PDF and view on different screen sizes.
5. **Test with ATS scanner** — upload the PDF to a free ATS checker before applying.

## What to Avoid

| Template Style | Why to Avoid |
|---------------|-------------|
| Infographic resume | ATS can't parse, creative but unusable |
| Two-column with skills sidebar | ATS parsing breaks, hard to scan |
| Creative/graphic-forward | ATS ignores graphics, content gets lost |
| Table-based layout | ATS fails on tables — content goes missing |
| Complex visual hierarchy | Recruiters scan, not study design |

## Before/After Transformations

| Before (Breaks ATS) | After (ATS-Safe) | Why Better |
|---------------------|-----------------|------------|
| Two-column infographic with colored icons | Single column, standard bullets, clean typography | ATS-safe, scannable |
| "My Journey" as section heading, fancy fonts | Standard headings, Arial/Calibri, single column | ATS can parse, professional |
| Logo with contact info inside | Text URLs for contact info in header | ATS reads all contact info |
| Custom diamond bullets | Standard bullet (•) | ATS recognizes it |

## Quick Reference

| Do | Don't |
|---|-------|
| Use single-column, standard headings, clean typography | Use creative headings, graphics-heavy layouts, custom symbols |
| Export as PDF, test with ATS scanner, check on mobile | Use tables, text boxes, decorative fonts |
| Choose one template and stick with it | Mix different template styles |

## Rules

- ✅ Use single-column layout throughout
- ✅ Use standard section headings
- ✅ Use Arial, Calibri, or Times New Roman fonts
- ✅ Export as PDF and test with ATS scanner
- ✅ Keep design conservative — content is what gets you the interview
- ❌ Don't use two-column layouts or tables
- ❌ Don't use decorative or script fonts
- ❌ Don't use creative section headings
- ❌ Don't use custom bullet symbols or colored icons
- ❌ Don't use infographic or graphic-heavy templates
