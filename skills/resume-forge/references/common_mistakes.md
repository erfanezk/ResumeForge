# Common Mistakes Deep Dive

Fix these 10 mistakes and your interview chances increase significantly. Each one is a silent resume killer.

## Why They Matter

- Even one mistake can get your resume auto-rejected
- Most mistakes are fixable in under an hour
- Eliminating mistakes separates you from 90% of candidates

**Rule:** A single critical mistake can sink an otherwise strong resume. Don't give recruiters a reason to discard you.

## The 10 Mistakes

### 1. Complex Language

Don't use words that are too hard to follow. Recruiters scan — they won't decode your resume.

| Before (Hard to Read) | After (Clear) |
|----------------------|---------------|
| "Executed a multitude of tasks pertaining to the optimization of manifold software paradigms." | "Improved software performance by 20%" |
| "Leveraged synergies to ideate efficient scalable solutions" | "Improved team delivery by adopting Scrum practices" |

### 2. Buzzword Overload

Natural language beats buzzword soup every time.

| Before (Buzzword Soup) | After (Natural) |
|----------------------|-----------------|
| "Leveraged agile methodologies in a cross-functional team to ideate scalable efficient solutions" | "Led a cross-functional team, adopting Agile practices to deliver faster" |
| "Synergized blockchain solutions for enterprise disruption" | "Built a supply chain tracking system using blockchain, reducing disputes by 40%" |

### 3. Unverifiable Metrics

Every number must be defensible in an interview. Don't inflate.

| Before (Unverifiable) | After (Defensible) |
|----------------------|-------------------|
| "Increased code performance by 200%" (no evidence) | "Optimized SQL queries by creating a caching layer, reducing load time by 50% — approved by A/B testing" |
| "Boosted revenue by 80%" (no context) | "Added a referral feature that increased monthly recurring revenue by 15% ($12K/month)" |

### 4. Unrelated Achievements

Hiring managers don't care about personal achievements unrelated to the job.

| Before (Irrelevant) | After (Relevant) |
|--------------------|-------------------|
| "Increased sales and turnover with business strategy" (for a developer role) | "Designed and implemented a caching solution that improved system latency from 800ms to 120ms" |
| "Won a baking competition" (for a dev role) | Omit or minimize to one line if demonstrates soft skills |

### 5. Invalid Technical Statements

Claims must be technically accurate. "Clean code" and "optimization" are different things.

| Before (Inaccurate) | After (Accurate) |
|--------------------|------------------|
| "Optimized code by following clean code principles" | "Refactored 15,000 lines of legacy code to improve readability and reduce bug rate by 30%" |
| "Improved performance using microservices" | "Migrated monolithic app to 12 microservices, reducing deployment time from 2 hours to 8 minutes" |

### 6. Not ATS-Friendly

A great resume that ATS can't read never reaches a human.

| Before (ATS Failure) | After (ATS-Safe) |
|---------------------|-----------------|
| "Front-end Developer" when job says "Frontend Developer" | Match exact casing: "Frontend Developer" |
| Two-column layout with skills sidebar | Single-column layout throughout |
| Custom section heading "My Toolbox" | Standard heading: "Skills" |

### 7. Poorly Written Skills

Listing skills you can't discuss in an interview sets you up to fail.

| Before (Too Many, Unfocused) | After (Focused, Defensible) |
|-----------------------------|-----------------------------|
| "Expert in Java, C++, Python, Machine Learning, MariaDB, PostgreSQL, MySQL, FastAPI, Django, SQL, Spring Boot, Docker" | "Python, Docker, Django, FastAPI, SQL, PostgreSQL" |

See the Skills Deep Dive for full guidance.

### 8. Generic Summary

Your summary should describe YOU — not any developer with your job title.

| Before (Generic) | After (Specific) |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "A passionate software developer with 5 years of experience who loves building great products and is a team player." | "A backend developer with 5 years building payment systems. Reduced settlement discrepancies by 35% through a real-time reconciliation engine processing 2M+ daily transactions." |
| "Experienced professional with strong skills seeking a challenging role." | "A Python backend engineer with 4 years in fintech. Built a fraud detection service that prevented $200K in monthly losses." |

### 9. No Bullet Points

Paragraphs = instant rejection. Recruiters scan, not read.

| Before (Paragraphs) | After (Bullets) |
|--------------------|-----------------|
| "At my previous job I was responsible for backend development and worked with the team to build APIs and also maintained the database and helped with DevOps tasks as needed." | "Built and maintained REST APIs serving 50K daily requests using Python and FastAPI" <br> "Collaborated with the DevOps team to containerize 8 services with Docker" |

### 10. Bad Structure

Non-standard formats confuse both ATS and recruiters.

| Before (Bad Structure) | After (Standard) |
|-----------------------|-----------------|
| Creative headings ("My Journey"), two-column layout, no summary section | Standard headings, single column, summary at top |
| Mixing education first for experienced dev | Work experience at top, education below |

## Quick Reference Checklist

| Mistake | Check |
|---------|-------|
| Hard-to-read language | Read aloud — would a non-technical friend understand it? |
| Buzzword overload | Count buzzwords — aim for 0 in final version |
| Unverifiable metrics | Can you explain the measurement method in 30 seconds? |
| Irrelevant achievements | Would a hiring manager for THIS role care? |
| Invalid technical claims | Would a technical interviewer agree with the wording? |
| ATS-unfriendly | Upload to a free ATS scanner before submitting |
| Skills not backed | Can you discuss each skill for 15 minutes in an interview? |
| Generic summary | Could 100 other people with your title write the same summary? |
| No bullets | Do you have bullet points instead of paragraphs? |
| Non-standard format | Are section headings standard? Is the layout single-column? |

## Rules

- ✅ Read your resume aloud — would a non-technical friend understand it?
- ✅ Count buzzwords — aim for zero in final version
- ✅ Every metric must be defensible with a measurement method
- ✅ List only skills you can discuss for 15 minutes in an interview
- ✅ Use ATS-safe formatting (single column, standard headings)
- ✅ Write bullets, not paragraphs
- ✅ Customize your summary per application
- ❌ Don't use complex language to sound impressive
- ❌ Don't stuff buzzwords — use natural language
- ❌ Don't include metrics you can't back up in an interview
- ❌ Don't list unrelated achievements
- ❌ Don't make invalid technical claims
- ❌ Don't use non-standard section headings
- ❌ Don't write paragraphs instead of bullet points
